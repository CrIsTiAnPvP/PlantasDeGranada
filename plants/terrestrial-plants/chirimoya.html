The Cherimoya (Annona cherimola)

BUY

The cherimoya is a very nutritious fruit mainly grown in tropical regions, such as the coast of Granada and parts of Málaga. Spain is the leading producer of this fruit in Europe, and Granada is one of the main regions where it is produced. The cultivation of cherimoya has increased in recent years due to its high demand in both national and international markets.

There are about 50 species of cherimoya, though only eight have commercial interest. They are typically identified by their color, size, and taste. The most common ones are green, gray, and red or pink, with the green ones having the sweetest and most compact flesh. It is a multiple fruit formed from several carpels of the same flower. Its skin is green, relatively fragile, and rough, with a pattern that looks like that of a reptile. The shape of the cherimoya resembles a heart, which makes it easily recognizable in markets.

Characteristics of the Cherimoya

  • Size and Shape
    • 5 to 7 meters in height
    • Oval or slightly irregular shape
    • Grows at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,000 meters above sea level
  • Leaves
    • Deciduous
    • Oval or elliptical, with smooth edges and a slightly pointed tip
    • Dark green color and soft texture
  • Fruit
    • Heart-shaped, weighing between 150 and 500 grams
    • Thin green skin, with a surface that may be smooth or have scale-like protuberances
    • Large black seeds, inedible and easily separable from the flesh

Edible and Non-Edible Types of Cherimoyas

Although many of these are not "cherimoyas" strictly speaking, they are species within the same genus and may be confused with them. It is important to know them to avoid risks. Some of them are:

Non-Edible Cherimoyas

  1. Annona reticulata (Reticulated Cherimoya)

    The seeds can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

  2. Annona squamosa (Sugar Apple)

    The atemoya, a hybrid between cherimoya and Annona squamosa, is edible but has no significant nutritional value. The non-edible parts, such as the seeds, should be avoided.

  3. Annona muricata (Soursop)

    It is part of the cherimoya family. Its seeds are toxic. Only its juice can be consumed.

Edible Cherimoyas

  1. Annona cherimola (Traditional Cherimoya)

    The classic variety that is the most common and found in many tropical and subtropical countries.

  2. Cherimoya Hybrids

    These are hybrids between different Annona species, such as the cherimoya and the atemoya (a cross with Annona squamosa), which are also edible. They are common in Asian regions.
