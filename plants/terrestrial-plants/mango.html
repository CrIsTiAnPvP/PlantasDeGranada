Mango (Mangifera indica)

BUY

The mango (Mangifera indica) is a tropical fruit native to South Asia, known for its sweet and juicy flavor as well as its high nutritional value. It is widely cultivated in many parts of the world, especially in tropical and subtropical areas.

In the Tropical Coast of Granada, the subtropical climate allows for the cultivation of this fruit, making it one of the main agricultural productions of the region, where high-quality varieties are produced.

Characteristics of the Mango

  • Size and Shape:
    • A tree that can reach 10-20 meters in height.
    • Round or elongated shape, depending on the variety.
  • Leaves:
    • Evergreen.
    • Lanceolate in shape, with a bright green color.
  • Fruit:
    • Variable size (200-500 grams).
    • Smooth and shiny skin, with colors ranging from green to yellow or red.
    • Large, flat seed, non-edible.

Benefits of Mango

The mango is a highly nutritious fruit, rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Some of its benefits include:

  • Rich in Vitamin C: Mango is an excellent source of Vitamin C, which strengthens the immune system and aids in iron absorption.
  • Antioxidants: It contains compounds like carotenoids and polyphenols, which help prevent cellular damage caused by free radicals.
  • Digestive Health: The fiber present in mango promotes digestion and may help prevent constipation.
  • Improves Skin Health: Thanks to its high Vitamin A content, mango helps maintain healthy skin and may improve its appearance by reducing wrinkles.
