What is Cystoseira?

BUY

The genus Cystoseira includes very characteristic brown algae found in Mediterranean waters, especially along the coast of Granada.

These algae are essential in the creation of underwater habitats, improving water quality, and providing shelter for many marine species.

Although not exclusive to this area, they are commonly found in the rocky coastline and seagrass meadows of the Alboran Sea.

Characteristics of the Cystoseira Species

Size and Shape

  • Size: From a few centimeters up to more than one meter.
  • Shape: Branched, resembling small underwater shrubs.

Structure

  • Stems: Main structures that serve as the "body" of the algae.
  • Airbags or Vesicles: Some species have floaters to keep them upright.
  • Color: Golden brown or dark green, depending on depth and light.

Habitats

  • They grow on rocky substrates in clean, well-lit waters.
  • Depths range from shallow waters to 50 meters.
  • They are sensitive to changes in water quality, acting as bioindicators of the ecosystem's health.

Ecological Importance

  • They provide shelter and food for numerous marine species.
  • They help oxygenate the water and control nutrient excess.

Cystoseira on the Granada Coast

Along the Granada coast, species such as Cystoseira tamariscifolia are common and form dense communities that provide great biodiversity.

Distribution and Habitats

Cystoseira species are distributed across various areas of the Alboran Sea, forming underwater habitats at different depths.

  • Rocky areas exposed to direct sunlight. Depths up to 20 meters.
  • Seagrass meadows in deeper, calmer waters, up to 50 meters deep.
  • Wave-protected zones, such as coves and bays, where water conditions are more stable.
