Posidonia oceanica (Seagrass)

BUY

Posidonia oceanica is a seagrass endemic to the Mediterranean Sea, of great ecological importance for marine ecosystems. It is found in sandy and rocky bottoms at depths of up to 40 meters, forming underwater meadows that are essential for marine biodiversity.

This underwater plant plays a key role in oxygenating the water, sediment retention, and providing habitats for various marine species.

Characteristics of Posidonia oceanica

  • Size and Shape:
    • Posidonia oceanica grows in the form of underwater meadows that can reach more than 2 meters in height.
    • Its leaves are long, narrow, and of intense green color, arranged in rosettes.
  • Leaves:
    • The leaves are elongated, up to 1 meter long, with a thick texture and resistant to water movement.
    • These leaves are key for underwater photosynthesis, providing oxygen and supporting marine life.
  • Reproduction:
    • It reproduces both by seeds and spores, and its flowers are small and barely visible.
    • The meadows of Posidonia can live for more than 1,000 years, creating a stable ecosystem for many marine species.

Distribution of Posidonia oceanica

Posidonia oceanica is common in Mediterranean waters, especially along the rocky coasts of Andalusia, such as in the Costa Tropical of Granada. It can be found from the surface to depths of 40 meters, creating an ideal habitat for many marine species, including fish, crustaceans, and mollusks.

Distribution of Posidonia oceanica in Spain

Region Estimated Area (km²) Comments
Balearic Islands 4,000 km² The Balearic Islands host the largest meadows of Posidonia oceanica in the Mediterranean Sea, especially in areas like Ibiza and Formentera.
Valencia Community 1,200 km² In the Valencia Community, Posidonia is mainly found in the Albufera Natural Park and along the southern coast of Valencia and Alicante.
Andalusia (Costa Tropical de Granada) 600 km² The coast of Granada hosts important meadows of Posidonia oceanica, especially in protected areas like the Alboran Islands Marine Reserve.
Region of Murcia 800 km² In the Region of Murcia, Posidonia is common in the marine areas of the Costa Cálida, especially near Mazarrón and Cabo de Palos.
Canary Islands 500 km² The meadows of Posidonia oceanica in the Canary Islands are smaller compared to those in the Balearic Islands, but they are equally important for local marine biodiversity.

Ecological Importance

  • Oxygenation of water: Posidonia produces oxygen through photosynthesis, improving water quality and the health of marine ecosystems.
  • Coastal protection: Its meadows help stabilize sediments, reducing coastal erosion.
  • Refuge for marine species: The meadows of Posidonia provide shelter and food for many species of fish and other marine organisms.

Posidonia oceanica in the Granada Coast

In the Costa Tropical of Granada, Posidonia oceanica is abundant in the clear and calm waters, especially around protected areas like the Alboran Islands Marine Reserve. Its meadows are essential for maintaining marine biodiversity and the health of coastal ecosystems.

The conservation of this seagrass is crucial, as it is one of the most important species for the sustainability of marine life in the region.
