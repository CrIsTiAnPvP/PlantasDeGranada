Common Daisy (Anthemis maritima)

BUY

General Information

Anthemis maritima, commonly known as the "marine daisy," is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. This plant is primarily found in coastal areas of the Mediterranean, including the southern coast of Spain. It is known for its ability to adapt to arid and saline soils, allowing it to grow in harsh environments near the sea.

Characteristics

Anthemis maritima is characterized by its white flowers with a yellow center, giving it a similar appearance to other species in the Anthemis genus. Below are some of its most notable features:

  • Height: Can reach between 30 and 50 cm in height.
  • Leaves: The leaves are thin and grayish-green in color.
  • Flowers: White flowers with a yellow central disk, grouped in flower heads.
  • Habitat: Prefers saline and dry soils near the sea, in rocky or sandy areas.
  • Blooming: Blooms mainly in spring and early summer.
  • Roots: Has deep roots that allow it to obtain water in arid soils.

Ecological Importance and Benefits

Anthemis maritima plays an important role in coastal ecosystems:

  • Soil Fixation: Helps stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion in coastal areas.
  • Shelter: Provides habitat for pollinating insects and small organisms.
  • Pollination: Its flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Uses and Applications

Although its use is not as widespread as other plants, Anthemis maritima has several applications:

  • Decoration: It is cultivated as an ornamental plant in Mediterranean gardens and coastal areas due to its drought resistance.
  • Medicinal Properties: Some parts of the plant have been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and as an anti-inflammatory.
  • Environmental Education: It is used in conservation programs and awareness-raising efforts about coastal flora.

Threats and Conservation

Anthemis maritima faces several challenges from human activity and environmental changes:

  • Urbanization: The construction of infrastructure in coastal areas reduces its natural habitat.
  • Tourism: Recreational activities, such as the movement of people and vehicles, can damage local populations.
  • Climate Change: Rising sea levels and extreme temperatures affect its growth and reproduction.

It is crucial to protect its habitat through regulations that limit coastal urbanization and promote the restoration of dunes and natural areas.

Information about Anthemis maritima in Spain and Granada

Location Habitat Conservation Status Blooming
Spain Mediterranean coast, rocky and sandy soils. Endangered in some areas due to urban development. Spring and early summer (April to June).
Granada Coastal areas of the Natural Park of Hacinas and surrounding areas. Relatively stable, but at risk from coastal urbanization. Blooms mainly between May and June.

Note: Although not considered a critically endangered species, its habitat on the coast of Granada is threatened by urbanization and climate change.

Curiosities

  • The genus name "Anthemis" comes from the Greek word "anthemon," meaning "flower."
  • Its flowers have been used as a symbol of resilience due to their ability to thrive in poor soils and extreme conditions.
  • In some areas, its potential for phytoremediation, i.e., the ability to clean contaminated soils, has been studied.
