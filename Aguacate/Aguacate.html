Avocado (Persea americana)

BUY

General Information

The avocado is a tree native to Central America and Mexico, belonging to the Lauraceae family. Its fruit, also called avocado or palta, is a berry with a rough or smooth skin (depending on the variety), a creamy pulp, and a large central pit.

Characteristics

  • Height: Can reach between 10 and 20 meters.
  • Leaves: Evergreen, dark green, lance-shaped.
  • Flowers: Small, yellowish, and hermaphroditic.
  • Habitat: Tropical and subtropical zones.
  • Blooming: From February to May.

Avocado Information in Spain and Granada

Location Habitat Conservation Status Blooming
Spain Costa del Sol (Malaga) and Costa Tropical (Granada), regions with subtropical climate. Its cultivation is expanding due to high demand. Blooming depends on warm temperatures and the absence of frosts.
Granada The most notable areas are Motril, Almuñécar, and Salobreña.

Nutritious Benefits

The avocado is known for its multiple health benefits. It is an excellent source of healthy fats, mainly monounsaturated fats, which help reduce bad cholesterol (LDL) and promote cardiovascular health. It is also rich in fiber, vitamins (such as vitamin E, C, B5, B6, and K), and minerals like potassium.

Culinary Uses

The avocado is a versatile ingredient in the kitchen. Some of the most popular uses include:

  • Guacamole: A traditional Mexican dip made with avocado, onion, cilantro, lime, and chili.
  • In salads: Avocado adds texture and flavor to fresh salads.
  • In sandwiches and toasts: Avocado is used as a healthy substitute for butter or mayonnaise.
  • In smoothies: Avocado provides a creamy texture and is ideal for nutritious smoothies.

Avocado Varieties

There are several varieties of avocado, each with its own characteristics. The most well-known include:

  • Hass: The most popular variety due to its creamy taste and dense texture. It has a rough skin that ranges from dark green to black.
  • Fuerte: Has smooth, green skin and is less oily than the Hass variety.
  • Pinkerton: With an elongated shape and rough skin, it is known for its smooth and tasty pulp.

Avocado Cultivation

Avocado requires a warm climate to thrive, with temperatures not dropping below 5 °C. It is ideal for well-drained, slightly acidic soils. Cultivation in Spain has expanded, especially in the Costa Tropical, where the subtropical climate favors its growth. Avocados typically bloom in spring, and the fruit takes between 6 to 9 months to mature.
